Core Values and Principles

Accuracy as a Foundation: Accuracy is about understanding the underlying relationships between the numbers. I prioritize rigorous validation and data governance because good insights come from good data.

Transparency and Integrity: I practice honest reporting with no p-hacking or cherry-picking. Transparency means sharing what was cleaned, kept, or discarded, and acknowledging data limitations.

Purposeful Questioning: I start with the right questions in mind to avoid collecting meaningless data, remembering Mark Twain’s caution: “Data is like garbage. You’d better know what you are going to do with it before you collect it.”

Context Matters Deeply: Data can mislead without proper context. Data doesn’t lie, but it doesn’t tell the truth either — it’s our job to find the real story.

Analytical Patience and Exploration: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is critical. Slowing down to understand data deeply improves analysis and impact.

Data-Driven Decision Making: I believe, as W. Edwards Deming said, "Without data, you’re just another person with an opinion." Data grounds decisions beyond gut feeling, revealing hidden inefficiencies and guiding better outcomes.
